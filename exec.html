Statement of forgone revenue

We are holding a finished asset off the books, and willing buyers are banking currency they cannot spend.

Ten completed decoration sets sit fully built and shelved. Their paid launch windows closed years ago, so today they earn nothing. Meanwhile players keep saving the in-game token they would use to claim them. The gap below is not a cost to build anything. It is revenue already in reach, waiting to be booked.

+$47,676
The bottom line, up front One backlog. One server. One year. The difference between leaving these sets frozen and graduating them at the pace new sets already ship. That is roughly 2.7 times the token revenue of letting them out in a single late burst, and it generalizes across the wider server base.
All figures illustrative, scope: token store revenue from the 10 older sets only, never total game revenue

V

The ask

No new build, no discount to premium, no change to live operations. One scheduling rule.
Recommendation

Tie graduation to the cadence that already exists: each time a brand-new set launches, the oldest shelved set graduates into the token store.

One in, one graduates, about every 2.5 months. Premium launches stay premium. The monthly pop-up pack re-releases stay intact. Only the 10 older sets ever graduate, oldest first, and the schedule is already on the calendar. This is a parked asset being switched on, not a project to fund.

A quiet secondary benefit: because a completed set grants a permanent boost to army size, steady graduation also gently narrows the gap for players who spend less, with no demand on anyone, simply a healthier curve.

The single question for the product team We have a finished asset earning $0 and buyers who want to pay for it. What is keeping us from graduating one older set on the cadence we already ship new ones?
The cannibalization question, addressed

The likely concern is that graduating older sets eats premium launch-pack sales. It does not. The premium windows on these 10 older sets closed years ago and earn $0 today, so there is nothing to cannibalize. The newest 5 sets stay premium and untouched by this proposal. And a reachable token-store goal is precisely what keeps players topping up at all. On this view the change grows revenue rather than competing with it.
