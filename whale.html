v.A fair concern, answered

"Wouldn't this cannibalize
premium launch packs?"

The worry If older sets graduate into the token store, players will wait for them instead of buying the paid launch packs.
Why it grows revenue instead

The 10 older sets we are asking for had their premium windows close years ago. They are not for sale anywhere today, and they earn $0 as they sit. Graduating them competes with nothing, because nothing is currently being sold.

The newest 5 premium sets stay exactly where they are: premium, paid, and untouched by this idea. The monthly pop-up pack re-releases stay intact too. We are only ever asking for the old backlog to move.

And here is the part that matters most to all of us: a reachable token-store goal is what makes us keep topping up at all. Take the goal away and the spend stops, as our own receipts show. Give it back and the card comes out again. This grows the pie. It does not split it.

vi.A gentle aside

A quieter benefit, offered softly.

Completed sets grant a small permanent boost to march size, the size of our armies. A steady trickle of graduations would, over time, gently narrow the army-size gap for players who spend less. We mention it lightly, as a kindness the cadence happens to carry, never as a demand. The case stands on its own as simple revenue.

Open the shelf.
We will gladly fill it.

Tie graduation to the new-set cadence you already run. Each time a brand-new set launches, let the oldest shelved set graduate into the token store. One in, one graduates. Premium stays premium. The pop-up re-releases stay. Only the 10 older sets ever move. That is the whole ask.

We are the players holding full wallets at a locked counter. We are not asking for a discount. We are asking for the door. Give us a goal we can reach, and we will keep the card out all year. The revenue is already here. It is simply waiting to be let in.
