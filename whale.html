ix.A fair concern, answered

"Wouldn't installments cannibalize
premium launch packs?"

The worry If players can install their way to a maxed level a few dollars a week, they will skip the premium launch packs and we lose the lump sums.
Why it grows revenue instead

The lump sums in question are cheques almost nobody writes. A several-hundred-dollar payment for a +2 march level is a yes from a tiny few. The installment path is not stealing those sales, because at that price for that reward, most of them were never going to happen. A small weekly pack toward a visible goal is additive demand, not redirected demand.

The newest five premium sets stay exactly where they are: premium, paid, and untouched by this. Their launch windows are still the place to buy them outright. The monthly pop-up re-releases stay intact too. Installment leveling only ever applies to sets already reachable in the token store, the older windows that closed years ago and earn about $0 today.

And here is the part that matters most: a reachable level is what keeps us paying at all. A spender with a goal pays about $5 a week, every week, for as long as they grind. A spender with no goal pays nothing. Installments turn a one-time cheque almost nobody signs into a recurring stream a great many of us sign gladly. This grows the pie. It does not split it.

x.A gentle aside

A quieter benefit, offered softly.

Every level grants a small permanent boost to march size, the size of our armies. A steady trickle of graduations, plus an installment path to grind levels, would over time gently narrow the army-size gap for players who spend less. We mention it lightly, as a kindness the cadence happens to carry, never as a demand. The case stands on its own as simple recurring revenue.
