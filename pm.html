Product review · Store monetization

The level-up almost nobody buys as a lump sum

A decoration set's max level is a permanent march-size boost a spender genuinely wants. Today the only way to it is a big launch-pack lump sum, roughly $350 to $875, a price almost no one pays. Let players reach that same max level through the Decor Set Store in small weekly token installments, about $5 a week toward a visible goal, and a wall almost nobody pays becomes sticky recurring revenue paid gladly week after week.

The blind spot

A level-up priced as a wall earns almost nothing

A plus two march-size level is exactly the kind of permanent power a spender wants. But the only path is a launch-pack lump sum of roughly $350 to $875, and almost no one writes that cheque in one go. So the level goes unbought, and the recurring revenue and retention it could carry never appear on any dashboard. No alert fires on a price point that simply converts no one.

Patient players bank free tokens with nothing to climb toward. Paying players, with no installment path, never start. The leveling line earns close to nothing while it sits.

The lever

Sell the same level in weekly installments

Let players grind a set toward its max level through the Decor Set Store, paid in small $0.99 token packs week after week. The same level a spender would never buy for $875 in one go, they pay gladly at about $5 a week toward a visible goal. The launch packs stay premium and untouched.

~$257 a year, every year, from one whale who keeps a level to chase at about $4.95 a week, on the illustrative model below.
02

Hypothesis

IF / THEN

If players can reach a decoration set's max level through the Decor Set Store in small weekly token installments, the $0.99 pack capped at 5 a week, then a level that almost no one buys as a $350 to $875 lump sum becomes recurring revenue, because a spender pays roughly $5 a week toward a visible goal gladly, week after week, without reducing premium launch-pack sales, since the lump-sum buyers were never going to pay the wall price in the first place.

Experiment design and rollout

A/B: lump sum versus installment

Control / A

Max level reachable only through the launch-pack lump sum, current setting. Measures the level that almost nobody buys.
Treatment / B
Weekly installments
Same level reachable through the Decor Set Store in $0.99 token packs, capped at 5 a week, toward a visible goal.
  • UnitServer cohort, or staged servers if a clean split is easier than a per-player split.
  • PrimaryRecurring installment ARPU, paying weeks per spender, and engaged weeks to a level.
  • GuardrailPremium launch-pack revenue for the newest 5 sets must hold flat.
  • Window2 to 3 graduation cycles, roughly one to two quarters.

Rollout plan

  1. Open the installment path
    Let the $0.99 token pack, capped at 5 a week, count toward a set's level inside the Decor Set Store.
  2. Keep a reachable set flowing
    Graduate the oldest shelved set into the store on each new-set launch, so there is always a set to level.
  3. Watch the guardrail
    Confirm premium launch-pack revenue holds while recurring installment revenue grows.
  4. Tune the Level 3 cost
    The Level 2 to Level 3 step is adjustable. Set it where the grind stays motivating for the two Level 3 sets.
09

Risks addressed

"Installments cannibalize the launch-pack lump sums."
The lump-sum buyers were almost never going to pay $350 to $875 in one go, so there is little lump-sum revenue to cannibalize. Older sets reach the installment path only after their launch windows have closed, and the newest 5 sets stay premium and untouched. This grows revenue rather than trading it.
Mitigated
"It dilutes the pop-up packs."
The monthly pop-up pack re-releases stay exactly as they are. Installment leveling runs through the token store alongside them. The two systems run side by side.
No change
"Levels could become too cheap or too fast."
The weekly pack cap, 5 packs and about $4.95, paces the grind by design, and the Level 2 to Level 3 cost is adjustable. Only the two Level 3 sets, Metal Music Fans and Sweet Haven, climb that far. The rest cap at Level 2.
Tunable
As a gentle side benefit, each level is a small permanent march-size bonus, so an installment path also narrows the army-size gap a little for players who spend less per week. This is a neutral fairness note, not a request, and it is fully optional to the revenue case above.
Bonus

A wall almost nobody pays, sold as a habit almost everyone can

No new art, no new code, no new system. Let players reach a set's max level through the Decor Set Store in small weekly token installments, instead of only through a launch-pack lump sum of $350 to $875 that almost no one buys. It turns a level that earns close to nothing into recurring revenue and engaged-spender retention, illustratively about $69,000 a year from a 600-player population that earns about $0 today. This is an easy win to capture whenever the team is ready.
