01

The two acquisition paths

SOURCE: IN-GAME ECONOMY

Every completed set grants a permanent march-size bonus, a gameplay stat, and the bonuses stack. Owning more sets quietly widens the gap between the top and the broad mid-tier. Keeping that mid-tier collecting and engaged is the commercial reason to widen access, not a complaint. There are exactly two ways to own a set.

The store path is the affordable lane. A full set is 11,340 tokens. Players earn about 300 free tokens a week and can top up: the best value is a $0.99 pack (120 tokens) capped at 5 buys a week, about $5 for 600 tokens. Larger uncapped packs run $4.99 = 300, $19.99 = 1,280, $49.99 = 3,280, $99.99 = 6,480. Free over about 9 months, or about 3 months for a steady $5-a-week earner. Level 2 still requires collecting a set three times, so levelling stays a paid motion.

The defect: of 23 sets, only 8 are in the store and they merely cycle on rotation. 10 older sets (released 2021 to 2024) are shelved with no affordable path at all: their launch window closed years ago and they never entered the store. A verifiable fact anchors this desk: not one of the nine sets released since December 2023 has reached the token store. The remaining 5 are recent and stay premium; this position does not touch them.

04

Cannibalization stress-test

PESSIMISTIC CASE

The fair objection: do free-earned token sets undercut premium pack sales? We load the worst-case assumptions and the position still clears.

Why pack revenue holds

  • Only the oldest graduates. Recent and current sets stay premium. The newest 5 are never in scope.
  • The token path still takes months. Impatient and competitive players keep buying packs to skip the wait.
  • Levelling stays paid. Level 2 needs three copies of a set; that motion is unchanged by graduation.
  • Pop-up packs bundle far more than the bare set: extra currency, speedups, and exclusives. Those keep selling.

Who graduation actually reaches

  • The patient earner who was never going to pay full launch price. Today they get nothing and bank stranded tokens.
  • Lapsing mid-tier players given a reachable goal, which lifts session frequency and soft-currency top-ups.
  • A converted token spender is incremental revenue, since the alternative is zero, not a lost pack sale.
  • The whale segment is untouched: under any path, top spenders still chase day-one completion via packs.
STRESS RESULT: even crediting the displaced-pack-sale objection in full, every graduated token sale is incremental against a baseline of $0 on shelved sets. The model's downside is bounded by zero; the upside is the illustrative +$47,676 backlog delta. Position survives the pessimistic case.

The position, one line
Each time a new set launches, about every 2 to 3 months, let the oldest shelved set graduate into the token store. One in, one graduates.
Keeps the newest sets premium. Keeps the monthly pop-up packs intact. Only ever moves the oldest set.
Build cost: $0, the sets already exist. Downside: bounded at zero. Illustrative upside: a recurring backlog token-revenue stream that today reads as nothing.

METHODOLOGY: 52-week per-player simulation summed over a tiered population. Constants: full set 11,340 tokens, 300 free tokens per week, 10-set older backlog. Tiers, as weekly paid tokens beyond free and cash: Free 0 / $0; $5 tier 600 / $4.95; $25 tier 1,880 / $24.94; $100 tier 7,080 / $104.93. Mix 55 / 30 / 12 / 3 percent. Steady graduation releases the oldest set on the chosen cadence, one set every 2.5 months at default; hold-and-dump releases nothing for about 10 months then all 10 at once with the give-up haircut applied to the buyer count.
