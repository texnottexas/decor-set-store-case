01

The leveling opportunity (the headline)

Completing every piece of a decoration set is Level 1: a permanent +2 march size, no placement needed. Each further level adds +2 more (L1 +2, L2 +4, L3 +6), and leveling up means collecting duplicate full sets. Today the only way up is an expensive, time limited launch pack, so for most players it simply never happens.

A +2 march size level up is something almost nobody will pay a $350 to $875 lump sum for. But a roughly $5 a week installment toward a visible goal is something a spender pays gladly, week after week: exactly the sticky recurring revenue and retention the current design throws away.

05

The completion stream, side by side

Illustrative, older backlog token revenue only

Leveling sits on top of a second, already validated stream: simply completing a set the first time. Modelled over one year across a default population of 600 active set buyers, the question is not whether to release the backlog but how to release it. Hold everything and dump it at once, or graduate the oldest set steadily. Steady graduation also keeps small completion spend flowing all year (free 1 set $0, $5 a week reaches 3 sets at about $203, $25 a week reaches 4 sets at about $424, $100 a week reaches 4 sets at about $525), with graduations about every 2.5 months.

06

Access is the prerequisite for leveling

A player can only grind a set toward its maximum level once that set is reachable in the store. So the older one in, one graduates rule, where the oldest shelved set joins the Decor Set Store each time a new set launches, is the supporting prerequisite that makes installment leveling possible. Top War has 23 decoration sets: some are reachable through tokens, the rest sit behind closed launch windows.

07

Objections, answered

Q

Will store installments cannibalize launch pack sales?

No. Older launch windows are already closed, so no current pack revenue is at risk. The newest 5 sets stay premium and keep their full launch and pop up pack revenue. Installments simply add a recurring stream that exists today only as about $0.

Q

Won't a $5 a week path just replace people paying the lump sum?

Almost nobody pays the $350 to $875 lump sum for a +2 march level up, so there is little to replace. The installment reaches the spender who was never going to pay full launch price but happily pays small weekly amounts toward a visible goal.

Q

Does this devalue the monthly pop up packs?

No. The pop up packs bundle far more than the bare set and stay exactly as they are. Leveling installments touch only sets already reachable in the store, years past their launch, and reward patient, repeated spending rather than a one time buy.

Framed constructively: this is about giving engaged players a visible goal to spend toward week after week, and turning a stream booked today at about $0 into steady, predictable revenue.

Recommendation

Let players reach a set's maximum level through the Decor Set Store, paid in small weekly token installments of about $4.95 a week toward a visible goal, rather than only via $350 to $875 lump sum launch packs. Zero build cost because leveling already exists, low risk because older windows are closed and the newest 5 sets stay premium, and an illustrative recurring stream worth about $69,000 a year that the current design books at about $0.
