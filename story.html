Top War · A Player Story for RiverGame

Nothing left
to chase.

A long time commander finished every set the store could offer, and the game went quiet. Here is their year, drawn out in numbers, and a gentle idea that could fill it back up.

Told by a Server 2864 player
1 Meet the commander

A collector with an empty wishlist.

Commander Birch

Server 2864 · playing since 2022 · mid tier spender
"I collect the pretty sets because they help my marches grow. But I have run out of things I can actually reach."

Birch is a made up handle standing in for thousands of steady, friendly players who keep a server alive. They are not a whale and not a free rider. They log in, they earn, and they like having a goal in front of them.

Decoration sets are perfect for that. Complete a set and it grants a permanent march size bonus, and the bonuses stack across sets, so a collector always has a reason to keep going. There are 23 sets in the game. Birch owns all 8 that the token store currently offers. The store just cycles those same 8 on rotation, so for Birch the shelves are bare.

The remaining 5 are recent sets, still sold fresh as premium packs. This story does not touch those. It is about the 10 older sets, released between 2021 and 2024, whose launch windows closed years ago and which never reached the store. A simple, verifiable fact frames the whole problem: not one of the nine sets released since December 2023 has reached the token store.

2 The tokens pile up

Earning with nowhere to spend.

The store currency is Decor Set Tokens. Birch earns about 300 free tokens a week, automatically, just by playing. When there is a set to chase, those tokens flow straight toward it. When there is not, they simply stack up and go stale.

That last line is the quiet part worth repeating. When Birch has already bought everything available, the store stops earning. The tokens keep coming and keep going nowhere. A motivated, willing customer sits idle, not because they refuse to spend, but because the shelf is empty.

3 The wall and the locked door

Two paths to a set, and only one is open.

Every set has two routes. One is fast and premium. One is patient and affordable. For the 10 older sets, the affordable door is locked.

Path one · launch packs
~$175
to reach level 1 during the launch window
  • A premium ladder: $4.99, $19.99, $49.99, $99.99
  • About $525 for level 2 (a set must be collected three times)
  • Time limited: when the launch window closes, this door closes too
Path two · the store
~$94 or less
at most in cash, and far less once free tokens count
  • A full set is 11,340 tokens of soft currency
  • Free for a patient earner in about 9 months, about 3 months at a steady $5 a week
  • The catch: for the 10 older sets, this door is shut

Top up options keep the patient path gentle: the best value is a $0.99 pack of 120 tokens, capped at 5 buys a week, about $5 for 600 tokens. Larger packs ($4.99 for 300, $19.99 for 1,280, $49.99 for 3,280, $99.99 for 6,480) have no weekly cap for players in a hurry.

So for the 10 older sets, the premium door shut years ago and the affordable door was never opened. Birch cannot buy them at any reasonable price. The result is not more premium spending. It is a player gently disengaging from the part of the game they enjoyed most.

Meanwhile the studio keeps the catalogue lively, shipping a brand new set about every 2.4 months. Recent gaps between launches were 48, 80, 73 and 93 days, roughly 4 to 5 fresh sets a year. The pipeline is healthy. The only thing missing is a graceful exit for the oldest sets.

6 The population view

Steady beats a big dump.

Zoom out from Birch to a whole server of set buyers. There are two ways to release the backlog. Hold everything and dump all 10 sets at once near year end, or let the oldest set graduate steadily, about every 2.5 months. The patient path keeps players engaged, and that engagement is where the revenue lives.

A dump floods players with more than they can act on, so most of it strands and a chunk of the audience simply checks out. A steady drip keeps a goal on the horizon all year long, which is exactly what turns a willing earner into a recurring small spender.

7 The single ask
One in, one graduates

Let the oldest shelved set step into the store with each new launch.

Each time a new set launches (about every 2 to 3 months), the oldest shelved set graduates into the token store. The newest sets stay premium, the monthly pop up packs stay intact, and only ever the single oldest set moves. It costs nothing to build, because the sets already exist.

+1 in → +1 graduates
A self clearing backlog that always keeps the freshest content premium and gives patient players a goal again.
8 The fair question

Won't free token sets undercut pack sales?

It is the right question to ask, and the honest answer is no. Here is why graduation reaches a different player than the packs do.

Only the oldest graduates
Recent sets stay premium. The packs keep selling exactly what they sell today. Nothing fresh ever leaves the premium shelf.
The token path still takes months
Reaching a set through the store is a patient grind. Players who want a set now keep buying packs to skip the wait.
Levelling still costs money
Level 2 means collecting a set three times. The store opens a door to level 1, it does not hand out maxed sets.
Pop up packs bundle far more
The monthly pop up packs wrap the bare set in extra value. Buyers there are paying for the whole bundle, not just the set.

Graduation reaches the patient earner who was never going to pay full launch price anyway. It does not move a single premium buyer to the slow path. It turns a dead end into a small, steady, recurring source of spend, and it keeps a softly widening power gap from leaving mid tier players behind.

Give Birch something to chase again.

The content already exists. The currency already flows. The willing players are already here, logging in and earning toward a goal that is currently locked away. One small, recurring graduation turns a quiet shelf into a steady, friendly source of revenue, and turns a drifting collector back into a happy one.

Thank you for reading, and for building a game worth collecting in.
